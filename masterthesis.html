About PyRADTEMCam 1.0

The problem... Thermography, using specialized cameras to capture infrared radiation, offers a non-contact way of measuring temperatures, crucial in fields such as agriculture and plant eco-physiology. By assessing plant temperature, their level of water stress or crop evapotranspiration can be determined, avoiding tedious and limited methods. The use of thermal cameras mounted on drones allows for efficient irrigation analysis, but precision is required as fluctuations during flight and calibration variations can affect measurement accuracy. This study focused on investigating and correcting these errors, proposing methods to mitigate microbolometer drift, establishing temperature references, and developing algorithms to adjust thermal images, with the ultimate goal of applying these corrections in the agronomic field.

In the project... four continuous temperature sensors were constructed, operating with LoRaWAN radio communication and equipped with geopositioning capabilities. A structure was designed to mount these sensors and the controller for transportation during flights. Additionally, four panels of different colors were constructed to obtain the temperature of specific objects stable during flight, enabling the subsequent correction of images by designing a radiometric calibration model. This model takes into account the atmospheric effect on the drone's thermal camera and the behavior of the drone's own camera. Since these are uncooled thermal sensors, they exhibit temperature fluctuations, which influence the values of the captured images.

Finaly, a calibration model was developed that reduced the temperature error from 6 degrees between the temperature sensors and the pixel values of the panels in the image to less than 0.5 degrees, making it feasible to start working with physical models using this type of cameras. This achievement represents a groundbreaking advancement, laying the foundation for new endeavors in thermography, agriculture, and drones, which had never been accomplished before.
